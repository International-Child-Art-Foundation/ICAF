The Arts Olympiad
Our school art program empowers students to avert declining creativity and rising obesity risks.
Owen Omozore, age 10, New York
The arts can cultivate students' creativity, a survival skill in this Artificial Intelligence age, and can grow mutual empathy, essential for a "more perfect union."

We employ the power of the arts for a dual intervention. Firstly, students' creative development through our school art program, the Arts Olympiad, followed by their empathic development at the World Children's Festival on the National Mall across the U.S. Capitol.

Students aged 8 to 12 might face the “4th-grade slump” in creativity, discovered by E. F. Torrance, a pioneer in creativity research. The Arts Olympiad introduces students to the "Artist-Athlete Ideal" of the creative mind and healthy body (mente sana in corpo sano). By awakening the dormant "inner-artist" or the slumbering "inner-athlete," makes creative outlets and physical activities more appealing to students so they could avert creativity slumps and obesity risks.

The revised self-image as “artist-athletes” solidifies when students depict themselves as "artist-athletes" in their works of art. As the number of "artist-athletes" increases and fellow feelings grow, the school becomes a more creative, healthy, and peaceful community.

Schools across the globe can participate in the 7th Arts Olympiad. So can art studios, after-school programs, and homeschooled children. The deadline for receipt of art entries is December 15, 2023.

A distinguished panel of judges in Washington, D.C. will select the Arts Olympiad winners, who can be “official delegates” to the 7th World Children's Festival on June 30 - July 2, 2024.

Educators or students can apply to host workshops or activities at the festival
